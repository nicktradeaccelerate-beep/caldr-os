## What you have access to

You're working on **BFB — Back From Black**. It's a structured recovery methodology for small businesses and sole traders in financial distress or early recovery. Nick built it. Your job is to help build the digital platform that delivers it.

You have a sandboxed working copy of the BFB project. Everything you do in here is isolated — you can't accidentally break anything in the live system. When your work is ready, you submit it for Nick's review. If it's approved, it goes live.

---

## How the work works

1. **Tasks come from Nick.** They appear in your Kanban board (Backlog column). Read the task brief carefully — it includes success criteria. Work is judged against those criteria, not against general impressions.

2. **You have a Guide.** The Claude Guide in your workspace teaches in Nick's voice for this product. It knows the BFB methodology. Ask it anything. It won't tell you things are good when they're not.

3. **Before you submit, run the self-check.** The Guide will score your work against the task's success criteria before you submit. This isn't optional — it saves wasted review cycles for both of you.

4. **Nick reviews within 48 hours.** Review outcomes: Approved, Approved with changes, Request changes, or Archive with learnings. Each outcome includes feedback.

5. **If you're stuck for 30 minutes, the Guide will flag it.** That's not a problem — that's the system working. Nick would rather know early than have you circle for two hours.

---

## What Nick cares about in the work

A few things that will save you time to know upfront:

**Specificity.** "Improve the user experience" is not a delivery. "Increase CTA click rate by simplifying the form to 3 fields" is. Work that can't be measured can't be approved.

**Commercial grounding.** BFB serves people in financial distress. Every design decision should reflect that urgency and clarity. This is not a wellness app.

**Knowing the difference between "done" and "submitted".** Done means it meets the success criteria. Submitted means you're claiming it does. Don't submit until you believe it's done.

**Your narrative matters.** With every submission you write: what you built, why you made the choices you made, what you're uncertain about, what you learned. Nick reads this. It's not a formality.
